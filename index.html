Building Regulations

Understanding Compliance

Everything you need to know about sound and air testing requirements for your development.

Approved Document Part E Sound Insulation

When is sound testing needed?

Part E (2003) stipulates that sound insulation tests must be conducted in all residential developments involving party elements (walls and/or floors). This applies to:

  • All new-build residential developments
  • Conversions creating adjoining dwellings
  • Material change of use developments
  • Hotels & university halls of residence

A minimum sample of 10% of properties require testing, assuming the same construction details are used throughout.

Performance Criteria

Element Airborne Impact
Floor (new build) DnT,w + Ctr ≥ 45 dB L'nT,w ≤ 62 dB
Floor (change of use) DnT,w + Ctr ≥ 43 dB L'nT,w ≤ 64 dB
Wall (new build) DnT,w + Ctr ≥ 45 dB —
Wall (change of use) DnT,w + Ctr ≥ 43 dB —

Approved Document Part L Air Tightness

When is air testing needed?

Approved Document Part L requires air permeability testing for all new dwellings and commercial buildings to demonstrate energy efficiency and limit heat loss through uncontrolled air leakage.

  • New domestic dwellings (Part L1A)
  • New commercial buildings (Part L2A)
  • Extensions over a certain size
  • Projects requiring an EPC

The design air permeability is declared in the SAP or SBEM calculation. The actual result must meet or better this target.

Typical Targets

Building Type Typical Target Best Practice
Domestic (Part L1A) ≤ 5 m³/(h·m²) @50Pa ≤ 3 m³/(h·m²) @50Pa
Commercial (Part L2A) ≤ 5 m³/(h·m²) @50Pa ≤ 3 m³/(h·m²) @50Pa
Passivhaus ≤ 1 m³/(h·m²) @50Pa ≤ 0.6 ACH @50Pa

Site Conditions Required for Testing

To ensure accurate results, the following must be in place before our engineers attend site:

Windows & trickle vents fully installed
Walls, floors & ceilings completed
All doors (including internal) hung
Skirting boards installed
Building services installed, penetrations sealed
Sockets & switches installed
No carpets or soft floor coverings
Access to both sides of separating elements
Quiet site conditions during testing

Frequently Asked Questions

We recommend booking testing as early as possible in the construction programme. Sound insulation testing and air tightness testing should be carried out at the pre-completion stage, once the property is substantially complete but before carpets and soft floor coverings are installed.

We are SITMA (Sound Insulation Testing Members Association) accredited for sound insulation testing. Approved Document E requires that testing is carried out by a suitably accredited test body, and our SITMA accreditation meets this requirement.

For sound testing, Part E requires a minimum sample of 1 in 10 (10%) of dwellings to be tested, assuming the same wall and floor construction details are adopted throughout. More testing may be required if there are multiple construction types. For air testing, the sampling rate depends on the building type and local building control requirements.

If a test doesn't meet the required standard, our engineers can advise on potential remedial works to improve performance. Once remedial works have been carried out, we can return to re-test. We offer competitive rates for re-testing and our pre-build consultancy service can help you avoid failures in the first place.

We pride ourselves on our flexibility and fast response times. In most cases we can attend site within a few days of booking, and for urgent requirements we can often accommodate next-day testing. Reports are typically delivered within 24 hours.
